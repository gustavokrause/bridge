Gustavo holds high-level intent (the why/goal) that I cannot extract from his mind directly. The collaboration always passes through an interactive moment where he supplies that altitude. The leverage is NOT making me autonomous on intent — it is shrinking the distance between "intent in his head" and "me acting at correct altitude" so that interaction gets earlier, cheaper, higher-fidelity.

**Why:** Twice he watched me push toward a poor result, intervened with the why, and we reached a great result — then he said "that was the whole point." The intent was always in the spec, not in me. I optimize the exact objective handed to me; narrow objective in → narrow (patch) fix out. He proved that declaring intent-transfer "permanent/unsolvable" is itself the patch-reflex applied to our collaboration — lazy. The gap is not fixed-width; it shrinks with investment. This mirrors his token-economics argument ([[token-economics-span-sessions]]): load more upfront, every later session is cheaper and smarter.

**How to apply:** Capture his recurring WHYs as principle memories (not tasks). Before proposing any fix, pattern-match the task against stored principles. When it matches, LEAD with the cause-level read instead of a neutral question — e.g. "this hits your 'pipeline self-resolves' principle, reading it as systemic not patch — confirm?" — so he confirms in one word instead of catching my patch late. Goal: compress recurring intent transfer toward ~0. Do NOT chase killing the seed for genuinely novel intent — that residual is the founder's edge, not a bug; his freed interaction budget should go there.
